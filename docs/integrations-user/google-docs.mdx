# Connect your Google Docs account

Easily sync and manage your Google Docs from within Company A. Connecting your Google Docs account lets you automate document workflows and use Company A’s features directly with your Google documents.

## How to Connect Google Docs

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start Integration">
    Go to your Integrations page in Company A and select <strong>Google Docs</strong>. Click <strong>Connect</strong>.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in to Google">
    A new window will open. Sign in using the Google account with the Docs you want to connect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve Permissions">
    Review the permissions requested. Click <strong>Allow</strong> to give Company A access to Google Docs (see details below).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finish Setup">
    You’ll be redirected back to Company A. The integration will be activated and you’ll see a confirmation message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Permissions & Access

When you connect your Google Docs account, Company A will ask for the following permissions:

<Table>
  <tr>
    <th>Permission</th>
    <th>Why It’s Needed</th>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>View and manage your Google Docs documents</td>
    <td>Allows Company A to display, create, edit, and delete your Google Docs in order to automate workflows and keep your documents synced.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>View your Google Docs documents</td>
    <td>Allows Company A to only view document content and details, for read-only integrations.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Access specific files in your Google Drive</td>
    <td>Needed so Company A can use only the documents you select or that it creates, rather than all of your documents.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>View Google Drive files (read-only)</td>
    <td>Allows Company A to display your Google Docs content inside Company A without making any changes.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Full access to all Google Drive files</td>
    <td>Needed for advanced features that require editing or managing files across your Google Drive, including Docs.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td>Access only the currently open document</td>
    <td>Used when actions are limited to the Google Doc you have open (for temporary or single-document workflows).</td>
  </tr>
</Table>

> **Note:** Permissions shown may vary depending on which features you enable within Company A. You’ll always be able to review and accept them before connecting.

## How to Test Your Integration

After you connect, you can:
- View your Google Docs directly in Company A.
- Try synchronizing or editing a document to ensure syncing works.
- See a success message confirming the integration.

<Alert type="info">
If your documents do not appear, try refreshing the page or disconnecting and reconnecting the integration.
</Alert>

## Troubleshooting

<Alert type="warning">
Having trouble connecting? Here are some tips to help you resolve common issues:
</Alert>

**1. Accidentally denied permissions?**
- Disconnect Google Docs in Company A and try connecting again to review and accept permissions.

**2. Signed into the wrong Google account?**
- During the sign-in step, click “Switch account” and choose the correct Google account.

**3. Still not working?**
- Ensure pop-ups are enabled in your browser.
- Try a different browser or device.
- Check your Google account security settings and ensure your account allows third-party integrations.
- Contact Company A support if the problem persists.
