# Connect Google Docs to Your Company A Account

Easily link your Google Docs account to Company A to sync documents, automate workflows, and get more done right where you work.

---

## 1. How to Connect Google Docs

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start the Connection in Company A">
    
    01. Go to the **Integrations** page or section in your Company A account.
    02. Find **Google Docs** in the list and click the <b>Connect</b> button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in With Google">
    03. A new window will appear—this is a secure Google login.
    04. If you aren’t already signed in, enter your Google credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Grant Permissions">
    05. Review the permissions requested by Company A.
    06. Click <b>Allow</b> to grant access and continue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connection Complete">
    07. You’ll be brought back to Company A, and your Google Docs integration will be active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## 2. What Permissions Are Needed?

When you connect Google Docs, Company A will request certain permissions so it can sync and manage your documents for you. Below are the details:

<Alert type="info">
<strong>Why do we need these?</strong><br />
Only the minimum permissions needed for Company A to perform your requested tasks will be asked. Read on for clear explanations.
</Alert>

### Permissions Requested

| Permission                                    | What It Lets Company A Do                                                |
|-----------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **View, edit, create, and delete all your Google Docs**  <br/> (*Full access*)  | Sync and update your Docs as you use Company A.                         |
| **View your Google Docs only** <br/> (*Read-only*)          | Display your Docs within Company A, but never change them.              |
| **Work only with files Company A creates or opens** <br/> (*Limited access*)      | Only see and manage files you pick for this integration.                |
| **View your entire Google Drive (read-only)** | Show all Drive files (Docs and more), but can’t change or remove them.  |
| **Edit your entire Google Drive**  <br/> (*Full Drive access*)           | Let automation act on any of your Drive files if you choose.            |

## 3. Testing the Integration

After connecting, you can:
- Check that your Google Docs now appear within Company A’s interface
- Try creating or updating a doc to confirm your setup

<Alert type="success">If you see your documents or changes syncing, your Google Docs integration is working!</Alert>

---

## 4. Troubleshooting Common Issues

**Problem signing in or granting permissions?**
- Make sure pop-ups are enabled in your browser
- Double-check your Google account is correct
- If you denied permissions by accident, try connecting again

**Already connected but not seeing your Docs?**
- Log out and reconnect your Google Docs account
- Refresh the page after linking

**Need more help?**
- Reach out to Company A’s support team, who can walk you through the process step-by-step.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is it safe to link my Google Docs account?">
    Yes. Company A uses Google’s secure sign-in (OAuth 2.0), so your password is never shared. You can always remove access from your Google Account settings at any time.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I choose what access to grant?">
    In most cases, Company A will only request the permissions needed for your selected features. When prompted, you’ll see exactly what is being requested, and you can decide to proceed or cancel.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
